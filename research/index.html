Research Projects

My research focuses on household welfare measurement, remote data collection methods, and gender outcomes in development contexts.

COVID-19, Food Security & Gender: Phone Survey Evidence from LMICs
3 Published · 1 Book Chapter Bangladesh · Kenya · Nigeria

A longitudinal phone survey study tracking household food security, employment, mental health, and gender outcomes across three rounds in Bangladesh, Kenya, and Nigeria during and after COVID-19. Examines who gets reached by phone surveys, how food insecurity and mask-wearing behavior evolved, and how women's vulnerabilities changed over time.

Validating Remote Food Security Measurement: The rCARI Study
Work in Progress WFP

A multi-country validation study comparing the remote Consolidated Approach for Reporting Indicators of Food Security (rCARI) against the standard face-to-face CARI methodology. Conducted across three countries — Myanmar, Ecuador, and the Central African Republic — in collaboration with WFP. Using household survey data collected in both remote and in-person modes, the study examines agreement across food consumption, coping strategy, and expenditure measures, finding that rCARI is context-specific and tends to report lower food insecurity prevalence than in-person methods.

WhatsApp & Women's Livelihoods: Digital Practices and Economic Participation
Report Caribou Digital

Examines how women across multiple countries use WhatsApp in their daily economic lives — analyzing barriers and drivers of adoption, and how the platform shapes pathways into formal and informal economic participation.
