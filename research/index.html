Research Projects

My research focuses on household welfare measurement, remote data collection methods, and gender outcomes in development contexts.

COVID-19, Food Security & Gender: Phone Survey Evidence from LMICs
Food Security Gender & Women's Empowerment Public Health COVID-19 Survey Methodology Bangladesh Kenya Nigeria

A longitudinal phone survey study tracking household food security, employment, mental health, and gender outcomes across three rounds in Bangladesh, Kenya, and Nigeria during and after COVID-19. Examines who gets reached by phone surveys, how food insecurity and mask-wearing behavior evolved, and how women's vulnerabilities changed over time.

Causal Identification of Remote Data Collection Bias in Food Security Measurement
Food Security Humanitarian Settings Survey Methodology Myanmar Ecuador Central African Republic Burkina Faso

A randomized experiment embedded in multi-country household surveys to causally identify how remote data collection modes affect food security measurement. By randomly assigning households to phone or face-to-face interview modes, the study estimates the causal effect of mode on reported food consumption, coping strategies, and expenditure outcomes — contributing to a growing literature on measurement equivalence in hard-to-reach populations.

Data Quality Assurance & Indicator Standardization — WFP
Food Security Data Quality Indicator Standardization Stata R SPSS Python Tableau ggplot2

A cross-country harmonization and data quality initiative at WFP to standardize how food security and nutrition indicators are calculated, cleaned, and reported across Country Offices. The project developed a unified analytical pipeline in Stata, R, SPSS, and Python covering 20+ corporate indicators — enabling consistent, comparable outputs for needs assessments and monitoring exercises across 20+ country contexts. Complementary work included an open-source R package for WFP-branded visualization and a corporate data quality framework covering questionnaire design, enumerator training, high-frequency checks, and cleaning procedures — now the institutional reference on WFP's VAM Resource Centre.
